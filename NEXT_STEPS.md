## 0. Orientation — what this repo is

An 11-script R pipeline (`run_all.R` → `scripts/00b` through `07`) that produces tables and figures for a Nepal MoFA policy brief. The brief argues that Nepal should embed remittance-disclosure provisions in its bilateral labour agreements (BLAs), using the Korea EPS corridor as the case study.

**The pipeline is file-based, not variable-based.** Each script re-reads its inputs from disk. The one cross-script variable is `USE_REAL_DATA` at the top of `run_all.R`.

**The committed `output/` files are the source of truth for the brief.** Do not silently regenerate them — see the critical warning in Section 1 below.

## 6. Brief structure (IFA format — non-negotiable)

```
1. Executive Summary   — 150 words. Opens with the recommendation, not background.
2. Background          — ≤400 words. Corridor scale, EPS programme, RPW absence, FATF hook.
3. Analysis            — 1,800–2,200 words. Three sub-arguments:
     Sub-arg 1: The cost finding (fig_korea_operator_costs)
     Sub-arg 2: The monitoring gap (fig2 + RPW absence)
     Sub-arg 3: The MOU gap and the precedent (fig6 + fig8)
4. Key Findings        — 4 sentences max. Direct answers to the research question.
5. Recommendation      — 3 actions, 1 mechanism, 1 timeline, 1 metric each.
6. Conclusion          — FATF cost of inaction; window is the current renewal cycle.
```

**Three recommendations:**
1. MoFA formally requests World Bank RPW inclusion of Korea→Nepal via diplomatic note. Metric: corridor added within 12 months.
2. MoFA proposes Paragraph [X] addition to EPS MOU via Para 20 mechanism at next Joint Committee: (a) remittance facilitation as shared goal, (b) HRD Korea includes licensed MTO info in pre-departure programme, (c) annual corridor data exchange, (d) NRB-BOK cooperation modelled on 2024 KPP Terms of Reference.
3. NRB issues domestic fee-disclosure circular requiring all licensed RSPs to publish flat fees and FX margins relative to mid-market rate — modelled on BSP MORB Section 298 (Philippines).

**Audience:** Career diplomats. Foreign Secretary / Joint Secretary level. They skim headers first, have 10 minutes. Translate percentages to NPR impact. No academic hedging in main text — caveats go in footnotes.
